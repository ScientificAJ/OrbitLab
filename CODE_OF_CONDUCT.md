# Code Of Conduct

OrbitLab is a student research and hackathon project. Keep collaboration respectful, evidence-led, and welcoming to people who are learning the astronomy, software, and deployment pieces at different speeds.

## Expected Behavior

- Be kind, patient, and specific when giving feedback.
- Critique code, documentation, data provenance, and scientific claims without attacking people.
- Preserve uncertainty: distinguish bugs, missing artifacts, unsupported data, and confirmed scientific results.
- Credit sources, datasets, model artifacts, and contributors clearly.
- Help keep public issues useful by including target IDs, product URIs, commands, logs, and environment details when relevant.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing private contact details, credentials, tokens, or local machine secrets.
- Misrepresenting BLS candidates, model scores, or demo outputs as confirmed exoplanet discoveries.
- Pressuring contributors to bypass provenance, licensing, or safety checks.

## Reporting

For normal project concerns, open an issue using the templates or follow [SUPPORT.md](SUPPORT.md). For security-sensitive issues, use [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md) and do not disclose details publicly.

Maintainers may edit, hide, lock, or remove comments and issues that violate this code of conduct. Repeated or severe violations may lead to blocked participation in the project.
